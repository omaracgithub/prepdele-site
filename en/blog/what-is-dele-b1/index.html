What is the DELE B1 and what is it for? — Complete guide 2025

Updated January 2025 · 10 min read

What is the DELE B1?

The DELE B1 (Diploma de Español como Lengua Extranjera, level B1) is an official certificate issued by the Instituto Cervantes on behalf of Spain's Ministry of Education. It certifies that the holder has an intermediate level of Spanish according to the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR).

Unlike other language certificates that expire, the DELE has no expiration date. Once you earn it, it's valid for life. This makes it a long-term investment for your professional career and personal development.

Who issues the DELE?

The Instituto Cervantes is the public institution created by Spain to promote Spanish language teaching worldwide. It is the only organization authorized to administer DELE exams. The diplomas carry the signature of the Instituto Cervantes rector and have international recognition among companies, universities, and public administrations.

DELE B1 exam structure

The DELE B1 exam is divided into four tests that assess fundamental communication skills:

Test 1: Reading comprehension (70 minutes)

Consists of 5 tasks with a total of 30 items. You'll read texts of various types (articles, advertisements, emails, instructions) and answer multiple-choice questions or match information.

Test 2: Listening comprehension (40 minutes)

Includes 5 tasks with 30 items. You'll listen to conversations, monologues, news reports, and phone messages, then answer questions about what you heard. Each audio is played twice.

Test 3: Written expression and interaction (60 minutes)

Comprises 2 tasks. In the first, you must write a text of 100-120 words (letter, email, or message). In the second, a text of 130-150 words (article, blog post, or short essay).

Test 4: Oral expression and interaction (15 minutes + 15 preparation)

Consists of 4 tasks performed in front of two examiners. Includes a prepared short monologue, a photo description, a conversation about an everyday topic, and a communicative situation simulation.

DELE B1 scoring system

The four tests are grouped into two blocks for grading:

  • Group 1: Reading comprehension + Listening comprehension (maximum 50 points)
  • Group 2: Written expression and interaction + Oral expression and interaction (maximum 50 points)

To pass the DELE B1, you need a minimum of 30 points in each group, which equals 60% of the maximum score. Having a high average isn't enough — you must exceed the minimum in both groups separately.

If you pass one group but fail the other, your score is not saved for future exam sessions. You'll need to retake the complete exam.

What is the DELE B1 used for?

Obtaining Spanish nationality

Since 2015, citizens from non-Spanish-speaking countries applying for Spanish nationality must prove at least an A2 level of Spanish. However, many immigration lawyers recommend presenting the DELE B1 because it demonstrates stronger language proficiency and can facilitate the process. Additionally, the DELE B1 may exempt you from taking the CCSE test (Constitutional and Sociocultural Knowledge of Spain) in certain cases.

University admission

Many Spanish and Latin American universities require a B1 or B2 level of Spanish to admit international students. The DELE B1 is accepted as proof of language level at most higher education institutions in the Spanish-speaking world.

Career opportunities

In the job market, the DELE B1 proves you can communicate effectively in Spanish in professional contexts. It's particularly valued in sectors like tourism, international trade, teaching, and social services.

Personal development

Beyond practical uses, preparing for and passing the DELE B1 gives you a concrete learning goal and official validation of your progress in Spanish.

Differences between DELE B1 and DELE B2

The jump from B1 to B2 is significant. While B1 certifies that you can handle everyday situations and express simple opinions, B2 requires you to argue in detail, understand complex texts, and communicate fluently and spontaneously.

In practical terms:

  • B1 requires approximately 400-500 accumulated hours of Spanish study
  • B2 requires approximately 600-700 accumulated hours
  • B2 vocabulary is considerably broader and more specialized
  • B2 grammar includes advanced subjunctive, complex conditional sentences, and reported speech

2025 exam dates and registration

The Instituto Cervantes organizes several exam sessions per year for the DELE B1. Typical dates are:

  • February: limited session at select centers
  • April: general session
  • May: general session (the most popular)
  • July: general session
  • October: general session
  • November: general session

Registration typically closes 3-6 weeks before the exam date. Check the Instituto Cervantes website or your local exam center for exact deadlines.

DELE B1 exam cost

The DELE B1 price varies by country. In Spain, the fee is approximately €130 in 2025. In other countries, it may be slightly higher due to local administration costs. Some centers offer discounts for students or early registration.

Note that the fee is non-refundable if you don't attend the exam, although in some cases you can request a session change with proper justification.

How to prepare for the DELE B1

Ideal preparation combines grammar and vocabulary study with intensive practice of the exam format. Familiarizing yourself with each test's structure and managing your time well are key factors for passing.

Practicing with real exam models will help you identify your weak points and build confidence. Written expression and listening comprehension tend to be the tests that require the most work for candidates.
